Welcome To TheCommunityEarth!

This document works as a semi-alternate tutorial to teach new players how to get started on TheCommunityEarth. There are currently no other guides like it out there, making this the go-to place for players interested in getting into TCE.

First off, you can connect to the 1.12.2 server through thecommunity.earth

#1.1: Managing Your Town

Town Permissions are one of the most confusing aspects of the game. The default configuration of the permissions permits Recruits to Build / Destroy , Members to Interact with Doors, Chests, etc , Co-Leader with the ability to basically do anything. You can change your permissions to allow allies to modify your Town Builds by doing /t set perm ally on , or to turn it off /t set perm ally off. You can also promote / demote members of your town using the GUI, giving or revoking permissions from them. We heavily recommend you explore the Town Settings GUI by clicking on the corresponding item in the Town GUI or doing /to . Not a fan of the GUI? Thats ok, /tow is a text GUI based version of this.

#1.2: Town Financials

Each town requires Upkeep to keep their town alive. Upkeep is calculated based upon the total number of claims a town owns and its population. To pay Upkeep, just deposit money into your town bank using /t deposit [Amount of $] and it will subtract upkeep from the town bank every Upkeep cycle. If there is not enough cash in the bank to keep all the land, the town falls into ruin and loses all its territory

#1.3: Joining a Town

To join a town instead, ask for somebody to invite you by doing /t invite [Your Name] . This will send you an invite asking you if you would like to join the town in question. Type /accept and you are in!

#2: Economy

TheCommunityEarth uses an economy system. To check your balance, do /bal. To increase your balance, you may sell items using the /sell command. However, we recommend you exercise caution while selling to avoid selling valuables. There are also multiple other methods of increasing your balance. Such as people paying you using /pay, Killing Players gives out 15% of their cash capped at 1000. You may also trade with other players using the /trade command.

#3: Quests

Quests on TheCommunityEarth grant quest points. Quest Points are form of non-transferrable currency that can unlock content such as Events, Weapons and more! Quests can also award you with in game currency.  To take a quest, go talk to Warps at spawn and warp to The Warps Catalog. After finding a quest to take, talk to the NPC to take the quest and follow its request! As a quick tip, many quests are redoable. We do highly suggest taking a few quests early on as they can give you a good boost in progress.
